About me

I am a postdoctoral researcher at the Machine Learning Genoa Center, in the Statistical and Computational Learning Lab led by Lorenzo Rosasco.

The bulk of my research activity is dedicated to the development of machine learning algorithms for high energy physics.

I received my PhD in Astroparticle Physics from SISSA (International School for Advanced Studies), under the supervision of Stefano Liberati. During the first part of my career I studied quantum fields in discrete Lorentzian geometries motivated by Causal Set Theory. I also worked on models of modified gravity inspired by different quantum gravity scenarios.
